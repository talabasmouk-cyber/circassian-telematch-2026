What is Telematch?

Telematch is an annual community sports event that brings together Circassians from all over to meet in Canada. Teams compete in a series of fun, spirited games that test teamwork, agility, and creativity — all while celebrating our shared Adyghe heritage.

More than just competition, Telematch is where friendships are forged, traditions are honoured, and our community grows stronger together. From youngest to eldest, everyone finds their place in the festivities.

The Logo Story

At first glance, you see a single unified form. But the longer you look, the more it reveals.

Read On

I  ·  The Foundation

The Crescent at the Base

The logo reads from the ground up, and that's intentional.

At the base, a crescent-shaped vessel cradles everything above it. This is the foundation: the community, the diaspora, the shared soil that holds people together even across continents.

· · ·
II  ·  The Figures

A Woman and a Man, in Balance

Rising from that cradle are two figures. Not identical, not interchangeable. Look closely at the left figure: a belt cinches the waist, the traditional mark of a Circassian woman. On the right, a bullet holder crosses the chest, the adornment of a Circassian man.

They are distinct, culturally specific, and deliberately placed in balance. Both reach upward, arms curving outward like wings, like open hands, like the posture of someone mid-dance.

· · ·
III  ·  The Flame

Fire as Life Itself

Between them and above them rises the flame. In Circassian tradition, fire is not just heat. It is life itself, hospitality made visible, the hearth that defines home.

Here, it also ties directly to the spirit of the Telematch: the spark of competition, the warmth of gathering, the light that draws people from far places to stand in the same circle.

IV  ·  The Bzhikh

The Trefoil at the Center

And threading through the center, from the flame down to the base, is the Bzhikh, the trefoil motif. This is one of the oldest symbols in Circassian embroidery, a vertical axis that binds the design together. It represents the three worlds: water, earth, and sky.

· · ·
V  ·  The Stars & Leaves

12 Tribes, One Sky

The stars in some variations arc overhead like a crown, or like the Adyghe constellation. 12 tribes, one sky. The maple leaves, when present, are not an afterthought. They root the whole image in its specific home: Canada, where this community grew new branches.

A Tree of Life

When you take it all in, the crescent below, the man and woman rising, the ancient trefoil anchoring the center, the flame ascending... What you're looking at is a tree of life. Roots in culture, branches in community, and a flame at the crown that keeps burning wherever Circassians are found.

This is a logo that asks to be read.

The Teams

Four Teams · One Heritage · Endless Spirit

Team 01

Badin-Oqwe

Бэдынокъуэ

From the Nart Sagas

Badin-Oqwe, the swift and sure-footed, was known among the Narts for his ability to cross impossible terrain — rivers, mountain ridges, and contested land — without faltering. His name became a byword for those who press forward when the path is unclear. In the sagas, he is often called upon when others hesitate, trusted to carry the team's intent across whatever obstacle stands between them and the goal.

Logo Connection: The arrows of the Circassian flag cross, suggesting forward motion and precision — qualities that define Badin-Oqwe. This team carries the yellow of gold: the gold of the flag, of value, of things worth striving for.

Team 02

Lhe-Psch

Лъэпщ

From the Nart Sagas

Lhe-Psch was the Nart whose voice could rally a scattered people. Where others saw division, he saw the thread that connected each warrior to the next. His strength was not in solitary prowess but in the ability to hold the group together under pressure — to remind each member what they were part of and why it mattered. The sagas describe his presence as steadying: when Lhe-Psch stood among you, retreat felt impossible.

Logo Connection: This team carries the red of fire and resolve. The crossed arrows in the logo speak to the power of united force — directed, purposeful, unwavering. Lhe-Psch embodies this.

Team 03

Sos-Riqwe

Саусырыкъуэ

From the Nart Sagas

Sos-Riqwe was the thinker among the Narts — the one who studied the stars, read the land, and understood that wisdom was a form of strength as potent as any blade. In the sagas, Sos-Riqwe is consulted before great undertakings, his counsel sought by heroes and elders alike. He represents the truth that communities survive not only through courage but through knowledge passed carefully from generation to generation.

Logo Connection: Blue like the sky and the depth of still water. Sos-Riqwe's connection to the logo lies in the stars — those three points of light that represent the unity and wisdom of the Adyghe people, watching over the competition from above.

Team 04

Sana

Санэ

From the Nart Sagas

Sana was among the nurturing figures of the Nart world — not in a diminished sense, but in the deepest one. Sana understood that strength flows from rootedness: from knowing who you are, where you come from, and what you are tending. In the sagas, Sana is associated with the earth, with harvest, with the kind of endurance that outlasts any single battle. Her name evokes something that persists — the green of the homeland, the tree of life, the thing you return to.

Logo Connection: Green like the Circassian flag itself, like the forests of the Caucasus. Sana's connection to the logo is to the very ground it stands on — the field of green that holds all else in place.

The Games

Compete · Celebrate · Connect

Core Games

Treasure Hunt лъапӀэ хъунщӀэ
  • Captains receive a list of items to find. The list will be sent to Captains around 6:00 am.
  • Teams compete to find specific items on the list within a specified time frame.
  • Referees count the items each team finds after the search time ends.
  • Teams earn points based on the number of items found.
  • Teams with 30 or more players at the start of the game will receive an additional 50 bonus points. This will be the first game of the day.
1st: 100 pts 2nd: 75 pts 3rd: 50 pts 4th: 40 pts +50 pts if 30+ players
The Bridge дэкӀыпӀэ
  • A group of each team compete to reach the endline while only stepping on tiles transferred from back to end.
  • Only the last player in line can pick up tiles and pass them forward.
  • All team members must cross the finish line to complete the race.
  • If a player steps on the ground, the team restarts from the beginning.
  • Four players from each team. Ages: 6–14 and 15+. Two races total.
  • Each team of players gets five tiles to use to cross the finish line.
1st: 40 pts 2nd: 25 pts 3rd: 15 pts 4th: 10 pts
Potato Sac Race
  • One player from each team per age group (6–10, 11–15, 16–30, 30+) competes to reach the endline.
  • Four races in total, one for each age group.
  • Players hop in a sack from start to finish line.
  • For ages 16–30 and 30+, players must complete two laps (start to finish and back to start).
1st: 30 pts 2nd: 20 pts 3rd: 15 pts 4th: 10 pts
Circassian Table адыге ане
  • Teams compete to move balls from one pile to their designated area using a communal table structure.
  • Coordination and communication are key — the table is shared and requires teamwork to manoeuvre.
  • The team that successfully moves the most balls wins.
1st: 40 pts 2nd: 25 pts 3rd: 15 pts 4th: 10 pts
Tug of War капсэ хьэблэ
  • Ten players from each team pull against another team in a classic rope tug.
  • The team that pulls the opposing team across the centre line wins.
  • Multiple rounds — teams compete bracket-style.
1st: 40 pts 2nd: 25 pts 3rd: 15 pts 4th: 10 pts
Word Builder Relay
  • Three main rounds. Teams race to build words under time pressure.
  • Each player runs to collect a letter tile and brings it back for their team to assemble.
  • Speed and vocabulary combine — the team that builds the word first and correctly wins the round.
1st: 40 pts 2nd: 25 pts 3rd: 15 pts 4th: 10 pts
Target Balls топ хыфӀэдзэ
  • Three sizes of balls go in three different target areas — each worth different points.
  • Players aim and throw from a set distance.
  • The team that accumulates the most points from successful throws wins.
1st: 40 pts 2nd: 25 pts 3rd: 15 pts 4th: 10 pts
Balloon Sandwich Back to back
  • Two players from each team stand back-to-back with a balloon pressed between them.
  • Pairs race to the finish line without dropping or popping the balloon.
  • If the balloon drops, the pair must restart from the beginning.
1st: 30 pts 2nd: 20 pts 3rd: 15 pts 4th: 10 pts
Dizzy Water Relay
  • Seven players per team (5 runners + 2 spinners).
  • Runners spin around a bat a set number of times then race to transfer water using a cup.
  • The dizziness adds the challenge — spill too much and your team falls behind.
  • First team to fill their container wins.
1st: 30 pts 2nd: 20 pts 3rd: 15 pts 4th: 10 pts

Additional Games (time permitting)

Moving Water Псы зэрызехьэ
  • Ten players from each team form a chain to move water from one container to another using only cups.
  • The team that transfers the most water within the time limit wins.
  • Spilling is inevitable — speed and coordination are rewarded.
1st: 30 pts 2nd: 20 pts 3rd: 15 pts 4th: 10 pts

Volleyball

Volleyball — Group Games Game 1 & Game 2
  • Badinqwe vs Sosriqwe — Game 1
  • Nartsana vs Telhepsch — Game 2
  • First team to reach 20 points wins; must win by a 2-point lead.
  • Games occur concurrently. Ages 16+.
Volleyball — Final
  • Winners of Game 1 vs winners of Game 2.
  • First to 20 points, must win by 2.
  • Power Play Card may be used up to twice, during the first set only, when the game is stopped. Benches one opponent player for the next two points.

Strategy Cards

Joker Card

Each team is entitled to one Joker Card during the event. When used, it doubles the points earned in the selected game. Must be declared by a co-captain before the start of that game. More than one team may use their Joker Card on the same game.

Wild Card

Each team may use the Wild Card once. It may be used on any game except the final game, declared before the game starts. Choose one of:

  • Gain an additional 10% of points earned in that game
  • Remove (bench) one specific player from a competing team for that game
  • Borrow one player from a competing team for that game
Nasty Card

Allows a team to deduct 10% of another team's points from a completed game. Must be used within 2 minutes after the end of that game.

Bounce Points & Prizes

Momentum Bonus

Teams that build momentum are rewarded:

  • Win two consecutive games → +5 bonus points
  • Win three consecutive games → +10 bonus points
Prizes
  • 1st Place Medals
  • 2nd Place Medals
  • 3rd Place Medals
  • 4th Place Medals
  • Most Valuable Player Award
  • Best Captain Award

Fines & Penalties

Fashion Violation
−10 pts per player

Removing a team shirt during the event is considered a lack of team spirit. Wearing another team's shirt also incurs this penalty.

Absent-Minded Captain
−5 pts

Applies when the captain is missing in action when called by the referee, or is clearly struggling to keep the team organised. Delayed responses, last-minute scrambling, or looking unprepared when leadership is needed.

Referee Intimidation
−5 pts

Screaming at, aggressively challenging, or arguing disrespectfully with referees. All teams are expected to respect official decisions in a sportsmanlike manner.

Excessive Celebration Before Confirmation
−5 pts

Celebrating a win before the result has been officially confirmed. Victory dances, dramatic shouting, or premature displays of triumph while the game outcome is still under review.

Under-Celebration Fine
−5 pts

Responding to a win with unusually low energy. Weak cheering, half-hearted applause, or delayed reactions. Enthusiasm and team spirit are expected and encouraged!

Disqualification During a Game
Disqualification + possible point deduction

Serious rule violations, unsportsmanlike conduct, or refusal to comply with official decisions. Additional point deductions at the referee's discretion.
